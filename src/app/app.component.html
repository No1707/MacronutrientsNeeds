Your macronutrients needs

Macronutrients are the nutrients that provide us with the energy (measured in calories) needed by our bodies for growth, metabolism and other functions. The prefix “macro” means large; macronutrients are needed in large quantities in contrast to micronutrients (vitamins and minerals).

Carbohydrates, proteins and fats make up the group of macronutrients. Let us take a moment to briefly look at each of these macronutrients.

Carbohydrates

These are a major source of energy for our bodies and are stored in our muscles and liver for later use.Foods that contain large amounts of carbohydrates include fruits, grains (rice, oats, barley, etc) and roots (potatoes, yams, carrots, etc).
1 gram of carbohydrate = 4 Calories

Our bodies use proteins to grow tissue and muscle, repair organs and to create hormones and enzymes; proteins are also used for energy when carbohydrates are unavailable. Foods that contain high levels of protein include poultry, fish, beans, diary, nuts and legumes.
1 gram of protein = 4 Calories

Fats

In order to absorb vitamins, our bodies need fats. Foods that contain a high percentage of fat include cooking oils, butter, nuts and cheese. Of the three macronutrients, fats contain the highest number of calories per gram.
1 gram of fat = 9 Calories

First, we need to determine your TDEE
(Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

Your total daily energy expenditure is an estimation of how many calories you burn per day when exercise is taken into account.

Determining your caloric budget (the number of calories you can consume) positions you to be more successful with your fitness goals; knowing your caloric needs empowers you with the knowledge to employ a more practical and sustainable strategy to achieving your fitness goals.
